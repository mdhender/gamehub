---
title: How Mining Works
weight: 30
---

Mining is how your colonies extract natural resources — metals, non-metals, fuel, and gold — from planetary deposits. Of all the production systems in the game, mining is the most straightforward.

## Mine Groups

Mine units are organized into **mine groups**, and each group is assigned to a single deposit. You can't split a group across multiple deposits, and you can't assign multiple groups to the same deposit. One group, one deposit.

A group can contain mine units of different tech levels. Higher-tech mines extract more resources per turn.

To assign mines to a deposit, you issue a **mining change order**. Before you can mine anything, you'll need to locate deposits using sensor probes.

## Steady Output

Unlike farms and factories, mines produce **every turn**. There's no growing season, no multi-quarter pipeline — just a steady flow of resources each turn. A mine unit delivers one-quarter of its annual capacity every turn, starting the turn after assembly.

Higher tech levels produce more: a TL-1 mine produces 25 mass units per turn, while a TL-10 mine produces 250.

## What Mines Need

Each mine unit requires **fuel** and **labor** every turn:

- **3 unskilled workers** (or equivalent automation units) per mine unit
- **1 professional** per mine unit
- **Fuel** — 0.5 units x tech level per turn

The good news is that fuel is allocated to mines and farms **before** factories. Your mines are the last production units to feel a fuel shortage.

## Shortages Are Gentle

This is where mining stands apart. If your mines don't have enough fuel or labor, output just **drops proportionally** for that turn. There's no penalty, no lost progress, no reset. The moment you restore inputs, production snaps back to full capacity.

Compare this to farms, where a single bad turn wipes out an entire year of progress. Mines are forgiving.

## Where You Can Mine

Only **surface colonies** can mine. Ships and orbiting colonies cannot.

Deposits can be found on:
- Surfaces of terrestrial planets
- Asteroids
- Moons of gas giants

You'll need to run sensor probes to discover what deposits are available before you can start mining.

## Planning Tips

- **Mine early.** Mines have no startup delay and produce every turn. The sooner you get them running, the sooner resources start flowing.
- **Match mines to deposits.** There's no point in having more mining capacity than a deposit can support. Check your deposit surveys before assembling mine units.
- **Fuel is rarely a problem.** Mines get fuel priority over factories. Unless your colony is severely short on fuel, your mines will keep running.
- **Automation frees workers.** Three unskilled workers per mine unit adds up fast. Automation units can substitute, letting you redirect population to other work.
